# Before/After Demonstrations

Annotated rewrites across content types. Use for output calibration.

## Example 1: Blog Post Introduction

### AI Original (Bad)
"In today's fast-paced digital world, maintaining consistent productivity can often feel like an uphill battle. Many professionals explore a myriad of time-management strategies, hoping to unlock their full potential. However, finding a seamless approach that integrates with a busy lifestyle is paramount for long-term success. Let us delve into the habits that can transform your workflow."

### AI Tells Present:
- "In today's fast-paced digital world" (banned opening)
- "myriad" (banned noun)
- "unlock their full potential" (banned phrase)
- "seamless" (banned adjective)
- "paramount" (banned adjective)
- "delve into" (banned verb)
- All sentences 15-22 words (no variance)
- Throat-clearing preamble, zero specificity

### Humanized Rewrite (Good)
"Most productivity advice is a trap. You download the apps, you color-code your calendar, and you wake up at 5 AM because a billionaire on a podcast told you to. But by noon, you're staring at a cold cup of coffee and a to-do list that hasn't moved. The problem isn't your discipline. It's your system."

### Why This Works:
- Opens with contrarian claim (6 words, high surprise)
- Specific details: "color-code," "5 AM," "podcast," "cold cup of coffee"
- Sentence lengths: 6, 25, 20, 6, 4 words (high variance)
- "But" creates conflict (South Park rule)
- Fragment for emphasis ("It's your system.")
- Zero banned words
- Zero em dashes

## Example 2: LinkedIn Post

### AI Original (Bad)
"Thrilled to share some insights from my recent career journey. While the path to leadership is often associated with technical skills, a crucial lesson I have learned is that fostering a culture of innovation is paramount. It is imperative to embrace every challenge as an opportunity for growth. By leveraging empathy and data-driven decision-making, we can unlock transformative results."

### AI Tells Present:
- "Thrilled to share" (sycophantic opener)
- "career journey" (banned metaphor)
- "crucial" (banned adjective)
- "fostering a culture" (banned phrase)
- "paramount" (banned adjective)
- "imperative" (banned adjective)
- "leveraging" (banned verb)
- "unlock transformative results" (banned phrase cluster)
- Relentless optimism, zero vulnerability

### Humanized Rewrite (Good)
"I got fired from my first management job. I didn't have a 'growth mindset.' I had an ego. I thought leadership meant having the best answers. It took two years and a lot of quiet rooms to realize it actually means asking the right questions. Don't try to be resilient. Just be useful."

### Why This Works:
- Status drop opening ("I got fired")
- "Wrong word" technique: mocking "growth mindset" with quotes
- Sentence lengths: 9, 7, 4, 9, 22, 6, 3 (extreme variance)
- Confession creates trust
- No transitions. Implication carries the logic.
- Ends on imperative command, not summary
- Zero banned words, zero em dashes

## Example 3: Email Newsletter

### AI Original (Bad)
"I hope this email finds you well. We are excited to announce the launch of our new analytics dashboard. Our engineering team has been working tirelessly to streamline the user interface. These updates underscore our commitment to providing a seamless experience. You can now delve deeper into your data. We believe this will empower your productivity."

### AI Tells Present:
- "I hope this email finds you well" (banned opening)
- "excited to announce" (sycophantic)
- "streamline" (banned verb)
- "underscore" (banned verb)
- "seamless" (banned adjective)
- "delve" (banned verb)
- "empower" (banned verb)
- Self-congratulatory tone

### Humanized Rewrite (Good)
"You asked. We listened. Finally. The old dashboard was clutter. It took too many clicks to find your monthly revenue, and frankly, it was ugly. So we burned it down. The new version isn't 'robust.' It's invisible. You log in, you see your numbers, you get back to work. No digging. No 'actionable insights.' Just answers."

### Why This Works:
- Opens with reader-centric framing
- Fragment trio: "You asked. We listened. Finally."
- Radical honesty: "it was ugly," "we burned it down"
- "Wrong word" technique: mocking "robust" and "actionable insights"
- Specific pain point: "too many clicks to find monthly revenue"
- Sentence lengths: 2, 2, 1, 5, 15, 5, 4, 10, 2, 3, 2 (extreme variance)
- Zero banned words, zero em dashes

## Example 4: Technical Explanation (Blockchain)

### AI Original (Bad)
"At its core, blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across multiple computers. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, linking them together in a chain. Because the data is distributed across a network, it is virtually impossible to alter. This transformative technology has the potential to revolutionize how we conduct transactions in the digital realm."

### AI Tells Present:
- "At its core" (banned opener)
- "transformative" (banned adjective)
- "revolutionize" (banned verb)
- "digital realm" (banned noun)
- Jargon stacking: "cryptographic hash," "decentralized digital ledger"
- Every sentence 14-22 words
- Defines by components, not behavior

### Humanized Rewrite (Good)
"Imagine a shared Google Doc that everyone can read, but no one can delete. Now, imagine that every time someone adds a line, it's written in permanent ink. If you try to rip a page out, the book screams, and everyone in the room knows you did it. That's the 'chain.' It's not magic code. It's just a way of agreeing on the truth without needing a bank to referee."

### Why This Works:
- Familiar analogy (Google Doc, permanent ink) instead of jargon
- "Screaming book" is viscerally memorable
- Defines by behavior (what happens when you try to cheat) not components (hashes, nodes)
- "Wrong word" technique: "magic code" in quotes
- Conversational tone: "That's the 'chain.'"
- Zero jargon. Zero banned words. Zero em dashes.

## Example 5: Personal Essay

### AI Original (Bad)
"Looking back on my journey, I realize that failure was a stepping stone to success. The experience of losing the contract was disheartening, but it underscored the importance of resilience. It forced me to look within myself and discover a newfound determination. I learned that when one door closes, another opens."

### AI Tells Present:
- "journey" (banned metaphor)
- "stepping stone" (dead metaphor)
- "underscored" (banned verb)
- "resilience" (banned hollow noun)
- "when one door closes, another opens" (dead cliché)
- Emotion labels instead of sensory evidence
- Disney ending

### Humanized Rewrite (Good)
"The phone call lasted forty-five seconds. They didn't want the contract. I didn't feel 'resilient.' I felt the blood drain out of my hands. I sat on the curb outside the coffee shop and watched a man try to parallel park a Honda for ten minutes. I just watched him. That was the reality of it. No opening doors. Just me and a gray Tuesday and the realization that I had to start over."

### Why This Works:
- Opens with specific detail ("forty-five seconds")
- "Wrong word" technique: dismissing "resilient"
- Physical sensation: "blood drain out of my hands"
- Irrelevant specificity: "man parallel parking a Honda" (proves lived experience)
- Anti-epiphany: doesn't resolve into a lesson
- Ends on grit, not hope. "I had to start over."
- Sentence lengths: 7, 6, 5, 11, 20, 4, 7, 3, 15 (extreme variance)
- Zero banned words, zero em dashes

## Example 6: Thought Leadership (AI in Healthcare)

### AI Original (Bad)
"The integration of artificial intelligence in healthcare represents a pivotal moment in human history. The synergy between AI algorithms and medical expertise underscores a transformative paradigm shift in diagnostics. By leveraging cutting-edge technology, we can seamlessly augment human capabilities, leading to more holistic and efficient patient experiences."

### AI Tells Present:
- "pivotal" (banned adjective)
- "synergy" (banned noun)
- "underscores" (banned verb)
- "transformative paradigm shift" (double banned)
- "leveraging" (banned verb)
- "cutting-edge" (banned adjective)
- "seamlessly" (banned adverb)
- "holistic" (banned adjective)
- Every word is a banned word

### Humanized Rewrite (Good)
"A radiologist looks at a thousand scans a day. By 4 PM, their eyes are tired. Their blood sugar is low. They are human. An algorithm never gets tired. It never misses lunch. We aren't building these machines to replace doctors. We're building them to catch the shadows that tired human eyes miss. It's not a 'paradigm shift.' It's a safety net."

### Why This Works:
- Opens with concrete subject (radiologist, not "integration")
- Specific details: "4 PM," "blood sugar," "misses lunch"
- Juxtaposition: human limitation vs. machine endurance
- "Wrong word" technique: dismissing "paradigm shift"
- Metaphor ("safety net") is fresh and accessible
- Active voice throughout
- Zero banned words, zero em dashes
