# Master Techniques: The 10-Master Craft Arsenal

These are the specific, actionable techniques drawn from master writers. Each technique targets a specific AI failure mode. Apply them in combination.

---

## 1. Provost Harmonic (Gary Provost): Sentence Music

**AI failure it fixes:** Rhythmic monotony. Sentences of uniform 15-25 word length.

**The technique:** Treat sentences like musical notes. Vary length deliberately.

**The rule:**
- Every 200 words must contain at least one sentence under 5 words
- Every 200 words must contain at least one sentence over 30 words
- Never write 3+ consecutive sentences of similar length
- Use fragments for emphasis. Single word? Fine.
- Use long, winding sentences that pile clause upon clause to build momentum and carry the reader forward through the logic of the argument until the period finally arrives like a breath.

**Example pattern:**
Short. Short medium. Long long long long long long long long long long long long long long long. Short.

**What Provost says:** "This sentence has five words. Here are five more words. Five-word sentences are fine. But several together become monotonous. Listen to what is happening. The writing is getting boring. The sound of it drones. It's like a stuck record. The ear demands some variety. Now listen. I vary the sentence length, and I create music. Music. The writing sings."

## 2. Clark Descent (Roy Peter Clark): Killing Abstraction

**AI failure it fixes:** Mid-ladder abstraction. "The employment landscape" instead of "a worker standing in the unemployment line."

**The technique:** Force every abstract claim DOWN the Ladder of Abstraction into concrete, sensory reality.

**The "For Example" Protocol:**
When AI writes something abstract, ask: What does that LOOK like? What does it SMELL like? SOUND like?

| AI Abstraction (Kill) | Human Concrete (Use) |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| "robust communication capabilities" | "You can drop it in a puddle and still text your mother" |
| "a nutritious meal" | "a peanut butter and jelly sandwich" |
| "office supplies" | "staplers, toner cartridges, and bent paperclips" |
| "the employment landscape" | "a worker standing in the unemployment line" |
| "a beverage" | "a lukewarm Bud Light" |
| "challenges in productivity" | "staring at a cold cup of coffee and a to-do list that hasn't moved" |

**Clark's rule:** "Get the name of the dog." Not "a pet." Not "a dog." The dog's name. The specific detail is what makes the reader see.

**Sensory injection targets:** Add at least one sensory detail (sight, sound, smell, taste, touch) per 300 words. AI has no body. It has never tasted coffee. Your text must prove a body wrote it.

## 3. Klinkenborg Severance (Verlyn Klinkenborg): Killing False Logic

**AI failure it fixes:** Over-connection. Drowning in "Furthermore," "However," "Moreover." AI glues every sentence to the next because it doesn't trust the reader.

**The technique:** Sever the connective tissue. Trust implication. Let sentences stand alone with white space between ideas.

**The decoupling exercise:**
- Find sentences joined by a transition word
- Delete the transition
- Make them separate sentences
- If the connection is obvious without the word, leave it deleted

| AI (Over-Connected) | Human (Implied) |
|--------------------|----------------|
| "The implementation was difficult; however, the team eventually adapted because they recognized the benefits." | "The new policy was hard. The team adapted. They saw the benefit." |
| "Furthermore, it should be noted that revenue increased. Additionally, customer satisfaction improved." | "Revenue went up. Customers were happier." |

**Klinkenborg's rule:** "Implication is the writer's most important tool." The reader's pattern-matching brain fills in the gaps. When you over-explain, you insult that brain.

**Kill the topic sentence:** Not every paragraph needs one. Let evidence speak. Start paragraphs with the evidence, not the thesis.

## 4. Zinsser Transaction (William Zinsser): Warmth and Agency

**AI failure it fixes:** Passive, noun-heavy, de-personalized prose. "The realization of goals is dependent on participation" instead of "The team must participate to finish."

**The technique:** Prune nominalizations. Restore active voice. Inject the first person.

**The clutter audit:**
| AI Clutter (Kill) | Human Clean (Use) |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| "The realization of the project goals" | "Finishing the project" |
| "make a determination" | "decide" |
| "give an explanation" | "explain" |
| "the standardization of the process" | "we standardized the process" |
| "It is believed that" | "We believe" |
| "Mistakes were made" | "We made mistakes" |

**The "I" Protocol:** Use first person where the content type allows. "I," "we," "us." Not "one" or "the author." Zinsser: "A writer is most natural and relaxed when writing in the first person."

## 5. Orwell Filter (George Orwell): Anti-Jargon

**AI failure it fixes:** Ready-made phrases, dead metaphors, Latinate bloat. Words that "think your thoughts for you."

**Orwell's Six Rules (adapted for AI decontamination):**

1. **Kill dead metaphors.** "Tapestry," "journey," "beacon," "game-changer" are dead. Replace with fresh images or plain speech. If you can't invent a new metaphor, use none.

2. **Kill long words.** "Utilize" becomes "use." "Facilitate" becomes "help." "Methodology" becomes "method." "Commencement" becomes "start." Anglo-Saxon root words hit harder than Latin derivatives.

3. **Kill filler.** "It is important to note that" becomes nothing. "In order to" becomes "to." If you can cut a word without losing meaning, cut it.

4. **Kill passive voice.** "It can be seen that" becomes "We see." "Is characterized by" becomes "features." Force a subject to own the verb.

5. **Kill jargon.** "Leverage," "paradigm shift," "sui generis" become "use," "change," "unique." Use everyday English.

6. **Break any rule sooner than write something dead.** If following a rule produces text that sounds mechanical, break the rule. The ear and the voice take priority over the algorithm.

## 6. Leonard Reduction (Elmore Leonard): Cutting Hooptedoodle

**AI failure it fixes:** Context dumps, throat-clearing preambles, over-explained setups. AI clears its throat for three paragraphs before saying anything.

**The rules:**
- **No weather openings.** "The sun shone brightly on the bustling metropolis" dies.
- **No prologues.** "In this article, we will explore" dies. Start with the content.
- **No adverbs in dialogue tags.** "He said gravely" becomes "he said." Or just remove the tag.
- **Leave out the parts readers skip.** If you see a thick paragraph that your eye wants to jump over, delete it.

**The Vonnegut Acceleration (companion rule):** "Start as close to the end as possible." Delete the setup. Start when the bear is eating the tent.

| AI (Throat-Clearing) | Human (Cold Open) |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| "Camping is a beloved pastime. However, it comes with risks. One must always be aware of wildlife. For instance, if a bear were to enter your campsite..." | "The bear shredded the tent." |
| "Marketing is essential for business growth." | "The biggest lie in marketing is that you need a bigger budget." |
| "In this lesson, we will explore the fundamentals of copywriting." | "Why do you scroll past 99% of ads but stop for that one sneaker ad at 2 AM?" |

## 7. Le Guin Leap (Ursula K. Le Guin): Pacing Control

**AI failure it fixes:** Uniform information density. Every sentence carries equal weight. No peaks, no valleys.

**The technique:** "Crowding and Leaping."
- **Leap:** Cut transitional filler. Jump over what the reader can infer. If AI uses 400 words, cut to 200. Force the narrative to jump.
- **Crowd:** Expand key moments with dense, specific sensory detail. What AI summarizes in one sentence ("it was an intense experience"), crowd with five sentences of visceral description.

**Le Guin's rule:** "What you leap over is what you leave out." Give weight to what matters. Skip what doesn't. AI gives everything equal weight because it can't judge importance.

---

## 8. Hale Verve (Constance Hale): Kinetic Verbs

**AI failure it fixes:** Static "couch potato" verbs. Is, are, was, were, has, have. AI uses linking verbs because they're safe.

**The technique:** Find "to be" and "to have" forms. Replace with verbs that punch.

| AI Static (Kill) | Human Kinetic (Use) |
|------------------|---------------------|
| "The company is a leader" | "The company dominates" |
| "There are many challenges" | "Challenges pile up" |
| "The situation is complex" | "The situation tangles everything" |
| "She was happy about the results" | "She lit up at the results" |
| "The market has been volatile" | "The market lurched and spiked" |

**Hale's rule:** Words should "vex, hex, smash, or smooch." Read aloud. If the verbs don't move, the reader won't either.

---

## 9. Prose Dissection (Francine Prose): Word-Level Scrutiny

**AI failure it fixes:** "Good enough" word choices. AI picks the statistically likely word. Humans pick the right word.

**The technique:** Interrogate every word. Ask: "Why this one?"

| AI "Good Enough" | Human "Right Word" |
|------------------|-------------------|
| "He looked at her with deep anger" | "He glared" |
| "She moved quickly across the room" | "She bolted" |
| "He felt sadness about the loss" | "He picked at the label on his beer bottle" |

**The gesture rule:** Delete the emotion label. Insert a physical gesture. Don't say "sad." Show the body being sad. Not "nervous." Show the knee bouncing.

**One word for three:** Find phrases that three words try to express. Replace with the single precise word. "Made a decision" becomes "decided." "Walked without purpose" becomes "wandered."

---

## 10. The South Park Rule (Trey Parker): Narrative Causality

**AI failure it fixes:** List-making disguised as argument. "X. Additionally, Y. Furthermore, Z." No causality. Just stacking.

**The technique:** Every sentence must connect to the next with "But" or "Therefore," not "And then."

| AI (List Mode) | Human (Causal Mode) |
|---------------|---------------------|
| "The company launched a product. Additionally, they hired new staff. Furthermore, revenue increased." | "The company launched a product. BUT it flopped. THEREFORE they pivoted, hired people who actually knew the market, and revenue tripled." |

**The rule:** If you can replace your transitions with "And then... and then... and then..." your writing has no spine. Stories need conflict (But) and consequence (Therefore).

## HIGH-VALUE COMBINATION TECHNIQUES (The Underground Hacks)

These techniques are the highest-impact differentiators. They're what separate "cleaned up AI" from "sounds genuinely human." Apply liberally.

### The "Wrong Word" Technique
Reclaim a buzzy cliché by putting it in quotes and dismissing it:
- "I didn't have a 'growth mindset.' I had an ego."
- "The new version isn't 'robust.' It's invisible."
- "We aren't going to talk about 'conversions.' We're going to talk about empathy."
This signals self-awareness. It proves the writer KNOWS the jargon and CHOSE to reject it.

### Radical Honesty / Status Drop
Start from failure, not success. Admit the flaw. Trust builds on vulnerability.
- "I got fired from my first management job."
- "The old dashboard was clutter. Frankly, it was ugly."
- "I spent $5,000 on ads last month. I got zero sales."
AI never starts with failure because RLHF rewards positive framing. This is the single most powerful anti-AI signal.

### The Zeigarnik Hook
Open a curiosity gap the reader must close by continuing:
- "Most productivity advice is a trap."
- "Here's the embarrassing mistake I made."
- "There's one thing every failed startup has in common."
The brain cannot leave an open loop. This is neurological. AI opens with context; humans open with tension.

### Irrelevant Specificity
Include a detail that's "off-topic" but humanly real:
- After getting bad news: "I sat on the curb and watched a man try to parallel park a Honda for ten minutes."
- The irrelevant detail is what makes it feel lived. AI never generates these because they aren't "logically" connected. That's exactly why they work.

### Negative Space / Framing by Negation
Define something by what it ISN'T:
- "We aren't building these machines to replace doctors. We're building them to catch the shadows that tired human eyes miss."
- "You don't need more strategies. You're drowning in strategies. You need a map."
Negation forces the reader to hold two concepts simultaneously. AI almost never frames by negation because it optimizes for direct information delivery.
