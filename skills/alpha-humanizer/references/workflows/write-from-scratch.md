# Write From Scratch Workflow

Generate new content that reads as 100% human-written from the start. Zero AI fingerprint.

## When to Use
User wants new content created (not rewriting existing text). Blog post, article, email, social post, essay, sales page, etc.

## Prerequisites
- Read `references/concepts/banned-patterns.md` (the kill list)
- Read `references/concepts/master-techniques.md` (the craft arsenal)

## Steps

### Step 1: Content Brief Extraction

If the user provided topic, audience, and content type, skip this step. Write.

If critical context is missing, ask ONE question that covers the gap:
- "What's this for and who's reading it?" covers content type + audience
- "What should the reader do or feel after?" covers angle + CTA

Never ask more than 2 questions. Produce a draft and refine from feedback.

### Step 2: Structural Blueprint

Before writing, decide the architecture. Never use the 5-paragraph essay or Hamburger format.

**For blog posts / articles:**
- Cold open: Start mid-thought, with a contrarian claim, or a specific scene. Never "In today's..."
- Body: Use the South Park rule (But/Therefore) not the List rule (Additionally/Furthermore)
- End: End on an image, a command, or a question. Never summarize.

**For LinkedIn posts:**
- Open with a confession, failure, or counterintuitive claim
- Tell a story with But/Therefore beats
- End with a single takeaway. No hashtag spam. No "Agree?"

**For sales copy:**
- Open with the reader's specific pain (sensory detail, not abstract)
- Agitate: Name the failed solutions they've already tried
- Present solution through outcome, not features
- Use bucket brigades between sections ("Here's the thing." "But wait." "It gets better.")

**For emails:**
- No "I hope this finds you well"
- Open with shared context or the reason for writing
- Body: One idea per email
- Close with a specific ask or next step

**For personal essays / memoir:**
- Open with a scene, not a reflection
- Use imperfective aspect ("I was sitting" not "I sat") for scene-setting
- Include irrelevant but humanly specific details
- Don't resolve everything. Real life doesn't tie up in bows.

**For technical documentation:**
- Imperative voice: "Click the button" not "The button should be clicked"
- Second person: "You" not "the user"
- Analogies for complex concepts (Orwell rule: "Imagine a shared Google Doc that everyone can read but no one can delete")
- No jargon stacking: define through behavior, not components

**For social media / threads:**
- Hook tweet: contrarian, specific, personal
- Story tweets: But/Therefore causality, not And Then listing
- Close: specific action, not generic inspiration
- No emoji as decoration. No 🚀🔥💡

### Step 3: Write the First Draft

Apply all techniques simultaneously:

**Rhythm:** Vary sentence length from the first paragraph. Mix 3-word fragments with 30+ word complex sentences. Never let 3 consecutive sentences land in the same length range.

**Specificity:** Every paragraph should contain at least one concrete, sensory, or named detail. Not "a drink" but "a lukewarm Bud Light." Not "challenges" but "staring at a cursor that won't move."

**Voice:** Write from a point of view. Have opinions. Use "I" or "we" where the format allows. Take a side.

**Verbs:** Favor kinetic verbs over linking verbs. "Dominates" not "is a leader in." "Crashed" not "experienced a decline."

**Transitions:** Use implication over connectors. If you must connect, use "But," "So," "And," "Still." Never "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally."

**Openings:** Start mid-action, mid-thought, or with a contrarian claim. Never with context-setting or landscape descriptions.

**Closings:** End on a concrete image, a command, a question, or a single potent sentence. Never summarize.

**Punctuation:** Zero em dashes. Use periods for separation, commas for pauses, colons for introductions, parentheses for asides.

### Step 4: Self-Audit

Before delivering, run through this checklist:

- [ ] Zero words from the Tier 1 banned list
- [ ] Zero banned transitions (Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, In conclusion, etc.)
- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] Zero throat-clearing openings
- [ ] Zero summary conclusions
- [ ] Sentence length standard deviation exceeds 10 words
- [ ] At least one sentence under 5 words per 200 words
- [ ] At least one sentence over 30 words per 200 words
- [ ] At least one sensory/concrete detail per 300 words
- [ ] At least one opinion, confession, or vulnerable moment per 500 words (in appropriate content types)
- [ ] No more than 20% of content in bullet/list format
- [ ] No Rule of Three (3 parallel examples of equal weight)
- [ ] No paragraph follows the Hamburger pattern (Topic-Support-Conclude) more than once
- [ ] Text includes contractions (can't, won't, doesn't)
- [ ] Active voice dominates. Passive voice under 15% of sentences.

## Output Format

Deliver the content directly. No meta-commentary ("Here's your humanized blog post:"). No preamble. Just the text.

If content is long-form (over 800 words), deliver in one continuous piece, not broken into labeled sections unless the content type requires it.
