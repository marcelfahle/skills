# Anti-Patterns: Pre-Delivery Verification

Run through this checklist before delivering ANY output. A single failure means the text needs revision.

---

## Category 1: Lexical Contamination

### The Residual "Delve"
**What it looks like:** A Tier 1 banned word from `references/concepts/banned-patterns.md` survived the rewrite.
**Why it fails:** These words are statistically verified AI markers. A single occurrence raises suspicion.
**How to avoid:** Check every Tier 1 word from the banned list before delivery. Zero tolerance.

### The Synonym Shuffle
**What it looks like:** Replaced "delve" with "explore" and "leverage" with "harness." The words changed but the voice didn't.
**Why it fails:** Surface-level word swaps don't change the cognitive architecture. The text still reads as AI with a thesaurus.
**How to avoid:** Don't just swap words. Rewrite the entire sentence from scratch. Ask: What is this sentence actually trying to say? Say that, plainly.

### The Tone-Deaf Replacement
**What it looks like:** Replaced formal AI words with casual ones, but the casualness doesn't match the content type. A legal brief that says "gonna" or a tech blog that says "utilize."
**Why it fails:** Mismatched register is as suspicious as AI-speak.
**How to avoid:** Match replacements to the target audience and content type. Professional doesn't mean stiff. Casual doesn't mean sloppy.

## Category 2: Structural Ghosts

### The Hidden Hamburger
**What it looks like:** Banned words are gone, but every paragraph still follows Topic Sentence + Support + Conclusion.
**Why it fails:** Structure is a deeper AI fingerprint than vocabulary. Word-swapping on a structural skeleton is putting a hat on a robot.
**How to avoid:** At least 30% of paragraphs must break the Hamburger pattern. Start some with evidence. End some mid-thought. Make some paragraphs a single sentence.

### The Invisible Rule of Three
**What it looks like:** No bullet points, but the prose still presents exactly 3 examples, 3 reasons, 3 arguments in parallel.
**Why it fails:** The Rule of Three pattern persists even without bullets. AI defaults to 3 across all formats.
**How to avoid:** Count your parallel elements. If there are exactly 3, change to 1 (vivid), 2 (paired), 4, or 5.

### The Rhythm Flatline
**What it looks like:** Sentence lengths are all between 12-22 words. No fragments. No complex sentences.
**Why it fails:** Low sentence length variance (standard deviation under 7) is the single most robust AI detection metric.
**How to avoid:** Must include at least one sub-5-word sentence and one 30+ word sentence per 200 words.

## Category 3: Tonal Artifacts

### The Residual HR Voice
**What it looks like:** Text avoids taking a position. Hedges everything. "While there are many perspectives..." and "It's important to consider both sides..."
**Why it fails:** Real experts have opinions. Real writers have stakes. Neutrality-to-a-fault is an AI signature.
**How to avoid:** Every piece should contain at least one clear opinion, position, or recommendation. Hedge less.

### The Disney Ending
**What it looks like:** Final paragraph wraps everything in optimism. "The future is bright," "paving the way," "a testament to human resilience."
**Why it fails:** Real conclusions are often ambiguous, challenging, or open-ended. Forced optimism signals alignment training, not thought.
**How to avoid:** End on a concrete image, a direct command, an unanswered question, or a gritty truth. Not hope.

### The Sycophantic Residue
**What it looks like:** "Great question!" or any variant of praising the reader/prompt before answering.
**Why it fails:** Immediate AI tell. No human writer opens with "Great question!"
**How to avoid:** Delete all reader-praising openers. Just answer.

## Category 4: Punctuation & Formatting

### The Em Dash Epidemic
**What it looks like:** Em dashes (—) appear in the output.
**Why it fails:** AI overuses em dashes 3-5x human baseline. Detection algorithms flag them. Even human writers now avoid them to prevent false positives.
**How to avoid:** Zero em dashes. Replace with periods, commas, colons, parentheses, or semicolons.

### The List Addiction
**What it looks like:** Content is 30%+ bullet points or numbered lists.
**Why it fails:** AI uses lists to organize information it can't synthesize into narrative. Lists signal "compilation" not "composition."
**How to avoid:** Maximum 20% list content. Convert lists to prose. If a list is necessary, make it brief and integrate it into surrounding narrative.

---

## Category 5: Information Architecture

### The Uniform Density
**What it looks like:** Every paragraph is 4-6 sentences. Every section is similar length. No crowding, no leaping.
**Why it fails:** Uniform information density is an AI signature. Human writing clusters detail at important moments and skips over transitions.
**How to avoid:** Apply Le Guin's Crowding and Leaping. Some paragraphs should be 1-2 sentences. Others should be 8-10. Important moments get more space. Transitions get less.

### The Context Dump
**What it looks like:** First 20% of the text is background, history, or definitions before getting to the point.
**Why it fails:** Vonnegut's rule: "Start as close to the end as possible." Preambles are AI clearing its throat.
**How to avoid:** Delete the first paragraph. If the piece still works, that paragraph was filler. Keep deleting until you reach the sentence that makes the reader say "wait, what?"

## Pre-Delivery Checklist

### Lexical
- [ ] Zero Tier 1 banned words (delve, leverage, tapestry, seamless, robust, etc.)
- [ ] Zero Tier 2 banned transitions (Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, In conclusion, etc.)
- [ ] Zero banned openings (In today's, In the ever-evolving, In the realm of, etc.)
- [ ] Zero banned closings (In conclusion, Ultimately, paving the way, etc.)
- [ ] Contractions present (can't, won't, doesn't, it's)

### Structural
- [ ] No 5-paragraph essay structure
- [ ] No Hamburger pattern in more than 1 paragraph per 500 words
- [ ] No Rule of Three (3 parallel examples of equal weight)
- [ ] No uniform paragraph lengths (vary from 1 to 8+ sentences)

### Rhythmic
- [ ] Sentence length standard deviation exceeds 10 words
- [ ] At least one sentence under 5 words per 200 words
- [ ] At least one sentence over 30 words per 200 words
- [ ] No 3+ consecutive sentences within 5 words of each other in length
- [ ] At least one single-sentence paragraph per 500 words

### Tonal
- [ ] At least one opinion/position/stake per 500 words (content-type permitting)
- [ ] No residual hedging ("It is important to note," "It is worth mentioning")
- [ ] No sycophantic openers ("Great question!")
- [ ] No Disney endings (forced optimism)
- [ ] Voice matches content type and audience

### Punctuation & Formatting
- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] Bullet/list content under 20% of total
- [ ] No excessive bolding (under 5% of text)

### Sensory & Specificity
- [ ] At least one concrete sensory detail per 300 words
- [ ] At least one specific name, number, brand, or time reference per 500 words
- [ ] Active voice dominates (passive under 15%)
- [ ] Kinetic verbs replace at least 50% of "is/are/was/were/has/have" main verbs
